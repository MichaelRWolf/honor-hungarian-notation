# I Have Come to Honor Hungarian Notation

> _After Mark Antony, Julius Caesar Act III Scene II_

---

Colleagues, coders, countrymen — lend me your eyes.
I have come to honor Hungarian Notation, not to mock it.
The prefixes that programmers add live after them;
The clear names are oft interrèd with their declarations.
So let it be with HN. The noble Practitioners
Have told you Hungarian Notation aids comprehension.
If it were so, it was a worthy gift,
And worthily hath every name been decorated.
Here, under leave of The Practitioners and the rest —
(For Hungarian Notation is an honorable practice;
So are they all, all honorable practices) —
Come I to speak in Hungarian Notation's defense.

It was my friend, faithful and clear to me —
in the days before compilers tracked our types,
before the IDE knew what our variables held,
before the language itself could tell us.
But The Practitioners say it serves us still,
And Hungarian Notation is an honorable practice.

It hath brought many mnemonics home to codebases,
Whose prefixes did the general comprehension serve.
Did this in Hungarian Notation seem obscure?
When that the programmer hath cried for context, HN hath answered;
Obscurity should be made of sterner stuff.
Yet The Practitioners say it aids us still,
And Hungarian Notation is an honorable practice.

You all did see that in your IDE
I thrice presented it a type annotation —
which it did thrice display without the prefix.
Was this a failure of comprehension?
Yet The Practitioners say the prefix was necessary,
And sure, Hungarian Notation is an honorable practice.

I speak not to disprove what The Practitioners say,
But here I am to speak what I do know.
You all did use it once, not without cause.
What cause withholds you, then, to abandon it? —
O judgment! thou art fled to brutish prefixes,
And programmers have lost their reason! — Bear with me;
My heart is in the codebase there with readable names,
And I must pause till it compile back to me.
